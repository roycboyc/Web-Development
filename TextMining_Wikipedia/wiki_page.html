<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Boroughs">Boroughs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Neighborhoods_in_New_York_City&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Boroughs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>


<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Borough_(New_York_City)" title="Borough (New York City)">Borough (New York City)</a></div>


<p>New York City is split up into five boroughs, which are <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Manhattan" title="Manhattan">Manhattan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queens" title="Queens">Queens</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Staten_Island" title="Staten Island">Staten Island</a>. Each <a href="/wiki/Borough" title="Borough">borough</a> has the same boundaries as a <a href="/wiki/County_(United_States)" title="County (United States)">county</a> of the state. The county governments were dissolved when the city consolidated in 1898, along with all city, town, and village governments within each county. The term <i>borough</i> was adopted to describe a unique form of governmental administration for each of the five fundamental constituent parts of the newly <a href="/wiki/History_of_New_York_City_(1898-1945)" class="mw-redirect" title="History of New York City (1898-1945)">consolidated city</a>.</p>
